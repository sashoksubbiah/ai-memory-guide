Inside the Embedding Space

What 1,536 dimensions actually means — and why the 2D visualizations you've seen are useful lies.

On this page
The dimensionsClusters and directionsWhy 2D worksChoosing a modelQuiz
The Dimensions

What 1,536 dimensions means

OpenAI text-embedding-3-small produces 1,536-dimensional vectors. Their large model: 3,072. These are not arbitrary — higher dimension generally means the model can encode more independent axes of meaning.

💡 Think of each dimension as

A dial measuring some abstract aspect of meaning. Not "noun-ness" or "positivity" — these are emergent, distributed representations. No single dimension corresponds to a human-readable concept. The meaning is encoded across all of them together.

Clusters and Directions

How meaning organizes in the space

Similar texts cluster near each other. You can observe this by projecting to 2D with UMAP or t-SNE — texts about cooking cluster together, away from programming texts, away from sport texts.

Certain directions encode semantic relationships. The classic word2vec example: king − man + woman ≈ queen. The direction from "man" to "woman" encodes gender as an applicable vector.

✓ Practical implication

Because meaning is geometric, you can do arithmetic on it. Average embeddings to represent a user's overall profile. Subtract topic vectors to filter noise. Combine query vectors to express compound intent.

Useful Lies

Why 2D visualizations work

Every 2D embedding diagram is a lie — a useful lie. You cannot faithfully project 1,536 dimensions to 2D without massive information loss. UMAP and t-SNE preserve local neighborhood structure but distort global distances.

These projections are still genuinely useful for sanity-checking, debugging retrieval failures, and communicating to non-technical stakeholders.

⚠ Do not optimize for 2D

Your retrieval runs in full 1,536D space. Optimizing embeddings to look nice in 2D projections may actually hurt retrieval quality.

⚠ Lock your embedding model

Once you embed your memory store, changing models requires re-embedding everything. Decide early and build a migration pipeline from day one.
